Bio

I recently graduated from Georgia Tech with a bachelor's degree in Computer Science, and am presently pursuing a Ph.D. in Human-Computer Interaction at Carnegie Mellon University. Broadly, my research interests are in creating better software that utilize mental models of users, streamlining authentication in cloud computing and ubicomp environments, and creating usably secure systems. Outside of these thrusts I am also keen on investigating how to leverage social media to (1) facilitate and enhance those aspects of human social behavior that are hard to do (such as maintaining old friendships and creating new ones) and (2) to create an ontology of popular opinion on the web for quick, at a glance, consensus. In fact, I have a couple of start up ideas that I'm presently pursuing in these domains.

I'm also interested in writing, travel, and playing sports. At Georgia Tech, I spent a year studying abroad in Singapore and spent a good portion of that time traveling across south east Asia. I was also part of the team that won the Gold Medal in Tennis at the Nanyang Technological University International Games. The following year, I worked for a company in Japan as a 3D modeler and animator. While there, I picked up a fair bit of Japanese (JLPT level 2), and learned my way around 3D Studio Max, Photoshop, Illustrator and After Effects. Oh, I've also been writing a fantasy novel for the past couple of years -- expect that soon (I hope)!
